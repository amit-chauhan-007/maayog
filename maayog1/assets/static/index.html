Hatha Yoga

There is more to yoga than the twisted postures, poses. Yoga is a deep-rooted science that revolves around the energies that surround us and the energies within us. To be in harmony with the universe and harnessing the external and internal energy to reach an optimal state of self and much more. Hatha Yoga is one such form of yoga that allows an individual to harmonise the solar ('Ha' meaning Sun) and lunar (“tha” meaning Moon) within us to reach a higher state of consciousness.

Hatha also means being adamant and hence Hatha Yoga in simple form means stepping out of your comfort zone and pushing oneself to do a little more, thus extending your boundary bit by bit. Therefore, Hatha Yoga is a more aggressive form of yoga where you stretch your normal limits to gain higher benefits. The benefits of Hatha Yoga are many - since it slowly balances your solar and lunar energies, which in turn boosts overall health. It helps to tone of the spine (which is a common problem today); it strengthens the muscles and improves flexibility, relieves stress, helps gain focus and improves concentration. These are just a few benefits of Hatha Yoga, a deep delve into this form will reveal many more benefits.

Our yoga teachers at the maaYog guide you through the various aspects of Hatha Yoga enabling you to go that extra mile.

Ashtanga Yoga

'Asht' meaning eight, 'anga' meaning limbs - Ashtanga yoga when literally translated means "eight- limbed yoga," as specified in the Yoga Sutras. According to Patanjali, the journey of internal
purification for the revelation of the Universal Self comprises of the following eight spiritual
practices:
Yama - moral codes
Niyama - Routine, self-purification and study
Asana - posture
Pranayama - control over breathing
Pratyahara - sense control
Dharana - concentration
Dhyana - meditation
Samadhi - being one with the Universe
The ultimate aim of Yoga is 'controlling the mind', the journey to which begins with the strengthening of the body.
As per the Yoga Sutra - Vama, Niyama, Asana and Pranayama are considered as external cleansing practices while Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana are internal cleansing practices. However if not followed in the right manner the internal cleansing practice can be dangerous. This the prime reason why a lot of emphasis is laid on the way 'Ashtanga Yoga' is performed and the Patanjali Yoga as laid out in the Yoga Sutra is the prescribed right form.
At maaYog, we lay great emphasis on proper follow through of each asana in Ashtanga Yoga, so that one learns the proper technique and lives a well-balanced life.Our yoga experts guide each session.

Sivananda Yoga

Sivananda Yoga is a form of hatha yoga that was founded by Swami Sivananda. This form of yoga was later made popular to the rest of the world by his disciple Swami Vishnudevananda founded the Sivananda Yoga Vedanta Centres in 1959. The Yoga centres were started with the aim of promoting the ancient science of Yoga for physical, mental, emotional and spiritual well-being. The Sivananda yoga comprises of an amalgamation of twelve asana's, which are carefully curated to increase the flexibility of the spine and strengthen it. The principal of Shivanada Yoga is based on five main beliefs: - Vegetarianism (right form of diet), Pranayama (breathing), Asana (posture/exercise), Shavasana (relaxation) and Vedanta (Positive Thinking)

Pranayam

Pranayama is the most common and easy to follow of all Yogic exercises. It is a form of yoga, which focuses on controlling the breath and channelizing it within our body. Our breath is the prime source of our life - 'prana'; it is the life force that drives us keeps our mind and body alive. 'Ayama' on the other hand means to regulate or control, therefore "Pranayama' is controlling or regulating of breath. There are various forms of Pranayama, which has different effects.

The regular and proper practice of Pranayama increases the quality of the life force – prana and enables one gain energy; it clears the energies around and clears the various chakras within our body. Pranayama helps create balance and harmony between your body soul and mind.
